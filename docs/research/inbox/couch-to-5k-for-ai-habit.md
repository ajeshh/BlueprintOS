# Claim: build an AI habit with a progressive daily practice ("Couch-to-5K for AI")

Hilary Gridley (behavior-change product work at WHOOP, Dropbox, Big Health) applies fitness/habit
behavior-change principles to learning to *build* with AI (updated 2026 to use Claude Code).

## Core assertion (the load-bearing claim)

*You don't master AI in a weekend any more than you get fit in one — what changes is your
**instincts**, not your knowledge. So build the skill the way Couch-to-5K builds running: a
**progressive daily practice**, <10 min/day, smallest-possible-first-step, small wins → confidence →
momentum. "Getting started is everything" (Pychyl); the common failure is taking on too big a step
and getting overwhelmed.*

Supporting moves / claims:
- **Distrust "I'm too busy."** Time is the stated blocker but rarely the real one; procrastination is
  waiting for an external force to change rather than changing your actions. Break the task into the
  smallest first step and take it.
- **Foundations > tools.** The workflows (e.g. "sharing folders of Markdown as context") will look
  cumbersome in 6 months, but the foundations don't change: communicating with AI, breaking down
  work, setting a clear quality bar, feeling out the "jagged frontier" of capability. "Keeping up"
  becomes mastery of **delegation + agent management**, not chasing models/tools.
- **The real payoff is ambition, not productivity.** After ~30 days the default flips ("How do I use
  AI for this?" stops being a question); you take on problems you wouldn't have considered, and "stop
  seeing yourself as the person who has to do all the work and start seeing your work as a machine you
  can build, improve, and run."
